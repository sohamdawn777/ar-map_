The Urban Pulse

Rashbehari Avenue is the lifeline of south Kolkata, where the hum of rickshaws and the chatter of street vendors form the city’s ever-present soundtrack. Among the bustling shops and food stalls, graffiti has found its space—slices of calm amidst chaos. Vibrant murals depict local folklore, Bengali cinema icons, and playful interpretations of everyday life. One wall illustrates the classic tram gliding through a storm of colorful patterns, while another portrays mythological creatures mingling with street cats—a nod to the city’s love for storytelling and whimsy. Historically, this avenue has been a commercial hub since the British era, and its street art reflects that blend of commerce, culture, and creativity. Graffiti here feels alive, like it’s constantly evolving alongside the city, capturing fleeting moments of Kolkata’s urban pulse.
